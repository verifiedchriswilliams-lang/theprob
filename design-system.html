%What's the probability?

The system behind the odds.

This is the source of truth for The Prob's redesign: the Clean Book direction. Colors, type, components, and voice, in light and dark. Everything here is backed by design-tokens.css, so it can be handed to a developer (or Claude Code) and rebuilt faithfully. Toggle the theme top-right to see every token adapt.

01 · The mark

The % is the brand.

The percent sign carries the whole thesis: What's the Probability. It's a probability made visible. It lives as a rounded brand chip beside the wordmark, stands alone as a favicon and app icon, and recurs as a sign-off on editorial Takes.

%The Prob
Chip + wordmark is the primary lockup. The chip alone is the app icon and favicon. Brand color fills the chip; the wordmark takes --ink.

◆ Do

  • Keep the chip square with a soft radius (--r-xl).
  • Use the brand color for the chip, white for the %.
  • Let it shrink to a 16px favicon and it still reads.

✕ Don't

  • Don't tint the % with a signal color (green/red).
  • Don't stretch, outline, or add gradients.
  • Don't pair it with emoji or other glyphs.
02 · Color

Ultramarine, with honest signals.

One brand accent, a deep blue-violet, kept deliberately distinct from the market signal colors. Green = YES / up, red = NO / down, borrowed from the markets so a reader can scan odds at a glance. These never get rebranded.

03 · Typography

Serif headline, clean body, mono data.

A three-voice system: an editorial serif for headlines (the opinion), a neutral grotesque for product chrome and reading, and a monospace for anything that behaves like a feed: labels, timestamps, tickers. Numbers run in the sans at heavy weights, always tabular.

The Hustle meets Bloomberg.
