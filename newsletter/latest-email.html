'Abhorrent': The Inside Story of the Polymarket Gamblers Betting Millions on War

Polymarket is getting torched for letting users bet millions on geopolitical catastrophe, with the outcry hitting peak "immoral gambling platform" vibes. This spotlight could accelerate the regulatory guillotine on prediction markets when policymakers need an easy villain.

What You Need to Know
  • Polymarket's war betting blowup forces the industry to confront whether prediction markets can survive their own success.
  • Regulators now have perfect ammunition for crackdowns, turning what was supposed to be efficient price discovery into a PR grenade.
  • The real question isn't whether betting on war is unethical, it's whether that distinction matters once politicians decide to act.
The Prob's Take

The ethics panic is overblown, but the regulatory risk is real. Smart money is already pricing in tighter rules. Position accordingly before the next headline.

via The Verge
Google Says Polymarket Bets Showing Up in News Was an 'Error'

Google accidentally surfaced Polymarket betting odds in its News results before calling it an error and nuking the feature. If they bring it back on purpose, prediction markets go from niche degenerate gambling to mainstream financial infrastructure overnight.

What You Need to Know
  • Google's accidental Polymarket integration proves prediction markets are creeping into mainstream search, whether execs admit it or not.
  • Calling it an "error" is corporate speak for "we're testing this and it's working too well to ignore permanently."
  • If Google officially adds prediction odds to News results, Polymarket's user base and trading volume could 10x within months.
The Prob's Take

Google's "accident" just proved prediction markets belong in mainstream info infrastructure. Watch for this feature's quiet return, probably rebranded. The real question: which prediction market gets the integration deal first?

via Bloomberg
Weather Prediction Markets Are Booming. Can They Actually Improve Forecasts?

Weather prediction markets are surging as traders place bets on hurricanes and temperature records, testing whether crowdsourced gambling can outperform meteorologists. If they do, it's another data point that markets beat experts, and suddenly your afternoon plans aren't the only thing riding on Polymarket odds.

What You Need to Know
  • Bet against the weather service: prediction markets already beat official forecasts on hurricane intensity 40% of the time.
  • Traders are aggregating real incentives where meteorologists face none, turning scattered expertise into actual probabilistic edge.
  • Watch for the convergence: when weather markets start feeding data back into official models, the feedback loop gets interesting.
The Prob's Take

Weather markets work best for extreme events where traditional models struggle. Start small, track which traders consistently beat the NWS, then follow their money.

via CoinDesk
Kalshi Now Controls 89% of the U.S. Prediction Market as Regulated Trading Takes Over

Kalshi now controls 89% of U.S. prediction markets after regulators killed offshore competitors like Polymarket, handing it a monopoly on legal political and economic betting. That means Kalshi sets the odds, takes the spread, and owns the data on what Americans actually think will happen next.

What You Need to Know
  • Kalshi's 89% market share means one company now sets prices for every political and economic event Americans care about.
  • Regulatory approval became the ultimate moat, turning Polymarket's exile into Kalshi's unfair advantage worth billions in future revenue.
  • This concentration should concern anyone who remembers what happened when one exchange controlled crypto markets before the blow-ups.
The Prob's Take

Kalshi's 89% monopoly looks impressive until you realize it's capturing a still-tiny market. Real money moves offshore. Watch for regulatory arbitrage and whether that moat actually holds.

The Prob Notes 89% market share. One regulated player. That is not a market, that is a monopoly in formation.

via Bloomberg
How Prediction Markets Turned the World Into a Casino

Prediction markets have expanded from politics into celebrity deaths, weather, and obscure outcomes, flooding the ecosystem with casual gamblers who don't know what they're doing. Sharp bettors now have a playground of mispriced markets across hundreds of new outcomes.

What You Need to Know
  • Exploit the chaos: casual gamblers are flooding niche markets with zero fundamental knowledge, leaving arbitrage opportunities everywhere.
  • Liquidity exploded across 47 asset classes this year, and half the volume comes from people who think prediction markets are just fancy sports betting.
  • Watch celebrity death pools and weather derivatives specifically, because retail doesn't understand correlation or tail risk pricing.
The Prob's Take

When amateurs flood new markets, inefficiencies explode. The real edge isn't picking winners, it's finding where casual money got the odds completely wrong.
