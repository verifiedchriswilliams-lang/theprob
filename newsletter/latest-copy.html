Weather Prediction Markets Are Booming. Can They Actually Improve Forecasts?

Weather prediction markets are blowing up as traders bet on hurricanes and temperature records, potentially crowdsourcing better forecasts than meteorologists. If they work, it's another win for the prediction market thesis that skin-in-the-game beats institutional expertise.

What You Need to Know
  • Bet on weather forecasts now, but don't expect markets to consistently beat the National Weather Service.
  • Track hurricane path predictions where real-time pricing data might actually reveal crowd intelligence advantages.
  • Watch whether casual bettors can outguess meteorologists who've spent decades studying atmospheric chaos.
The Prob's Take

Weather markets work best on events with clear settlement criteria and competitive edges. Avoid betting against meteorological agencies on their home turf, but exploit mispricing on secondary events like precipitation thresholds where crowd sentiment diverges from model consensus.

Kalshi Now Controls 89% of the U.S. Prediction Market as Regulated Trading Takes Over

Kalshi now controls 89% of U.S. prediction markets after regulators cracked down on offshore competitors like Polymarket. This regulatory monopoly gives one platform massive pricing power and the ability to set terms for how millions of Americans will bet on elections, rates, and other outcomes.

What You Need to Know
  • Kalshi's 89% market share proves regulation kills competition, not innovation, as offshore players scramble for scraps.
  • Watch for Kalshi to raise fees aggressively now that they've eliminated pricing pressure from serious competitors.
  • Congress will eventually notice one platform controls all prediction market data, triggering antitrust questions they can't ignore.
The Prob's Take

Kalshi's 89% grip means you're watching a regulated monopoly price elections and Fed moves. That moat is real, but it's also a target for the next competitor willing to sweat regulatory arbitrage.

How Prediction Markets Turned the World Into a Casino

Prediction markets have sprawled from politics and finance into celebrity gossip and weather, pumping liquidity into a massive betting ecosystem. The influx of casual players who don't know what they're doing just handed sharp bettors a gift: underpriced edges everywhere.

What You Need to Know
  • Exploit the dumb money flooding into celebrity death pools and weather derivatives with actual edge.
  • $500B in annual prediction market volume means real traders finally have the liquidity to move positions.
  • Watch casual gamblers misprice tail risks in non-traditional markets, then harvest their overconfidence for profit.
The Prob's Take

The algos are eating well on tourist money. If you can spot the difference between a mispriced tail event and pure speculation, you're printing.
