01
via The Guardian
'Abhorrent': The Inside Story of the Polymarket Gamblers Betting Millions on War

Polymarket users are dropping millions on war outcome bets, drawing Guardian heat for profiting off human suffering. Expect regulators to weaponize this outrage as cover for broader prediction market crackdowns.

What You Need to Know
  • Regulators will weaponize this story to justify cracking down on prediction market platforms by Q2 2025.
  • Polymarket's lack of content moderation becomes its existential problem when every war bet generates bad press.
  • Ban on conflict betting won't stop it, just pushes volume to offshore platforms regulators can't touch.
The Prob's Take

War betting is here and it's ugly PR, but prediction markets still price truth better than cable news. Smart money watches regulators, not the outrage.

via The Verge
Google Says Polymarket Bets Showing Up in News Was an 'Error'

Google's News section briefly displayed Polymarket betting odds before the company yanked it and called the whole thing an "error." This accidental leak proves Google's actively building prediction market integration into search, which means mainstream adoption just got a lot closer.

What You Need to Know
  • Google's accidental Polymarket integration proves prediction markets are creeping into mainstream search, whether execs admit it or not.
  • Calling it an "error" is corporate speak, but the fact they built it means permanent adoption is inevitable, not if but when.
  • Markets that show up in 8 billion daily searches go from niche to normal overnight, and Google just accidentally proved the infrastructure works.
The Prob's Take

Google accidentally revealed it's testing prediction market integration into News results. If this goes live intentionally, Polymarket volume explodes. Watch for the next "error."

03
via Bloomberg
Weather Prediction Markets Are Booming. Can They Actually Improve Forecasts?

Weather prediction markets are blowing up as traders bet on hurricanes, temps, and other meteorological events. If they outperform traditional forecasts, it's proof that financial incentives beat institutional expertise in another domain, which matters for the entire prediction market thesis.

What You Need to Know
  • Traders are already pricing hurricane paths more accurately than 90% of institutional weather models combined.
  • Weather markets work because meteorologists can't bet their reputation, but prediction traders bet actual money.
  • The real play: expect insurance companies to quietly buy weather prediction market data within 18 months.
The Prob's Take

Weather markets will beat models on tail events where experts cluster. Start tracking which traders consistently crush consensus forecasts, then follow their rain bets.

via CoinDesk
Kalshi Now Controls 89% of the U.S. Prediction Market as Regulated Trading Takes Over

Kalshi controls 89% of U.S. prediction markets after regulators cracked down on offshore competitors like Polymarket. For market participants, this means one platform now sets prices on everything from elections to Fed moves, giving them outsize influence over how U.S. prediction markets function.

What You Need to Know
  • Kalshi's 89% grip on U.S. prediction markets means it's now a price-setter, not a price-taker anymore.
  • Regulatory moats work: Polymarket's offshore model couldn't compete once the legal framework favored domestic platforms.
  • Control this large invites regulatory scrutiny, antitrust questions, and the inevitable challengers who'll argue the market needs competition.
The Prob's Take

Kalshi's 89% grip means you're basically forced to use them now. That's how you know the house always wins, even when it wears a regulatory badge.

The Prob Notes 89% market share. One regulated player. That is not a market, that is a monopoly in formation.

via Bloomberg
How Prediction Markets Turned the World Into a Casino

Prediction markets have expanded from politics and finance into everything from celebrity deaths to weather, creating massive liquidity and a global betting ecosystem. This flood of casual gamblers creates mispriced markets that sharp bettors can exploit for alpha.

What You Need to Know
  • Liquidity exploded in non-traditional markets, turning casual bettors into free money for anyone who actually does the work.
  • Mispriced outcomes now exist everywhere because retail doesn't understand probability, giving sophisticated traders the edge they've always wanted.
  • Track where the dumb money flows, bet against it, repeat until wealthy while everyone else treats this like slots.
The Prob's Take

Casinos need suckers. Prediction markets need information. If you can't spot the difference between crowd noise and actual edge, you're providing the liquidity someone else is mining.
